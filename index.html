SouL

Music programming language

Semester-long project for COMS W4115: Programming Languages and Translators. Created by myself, Matt Kim, Kevin Walters, and Cindy Long under the supervision of Professor Alfred Aho, SouL is a language designed to make it easy to work with MIDI data to create music and other sounds.

Thrown Controllers

Panel-based game show for video game trivia

An adaptation of the panel-based game show done by The Runaway Guys at PAX East 2013, Thrown Controllers is a game where two teams compete in a series of video game trivia challenges and multiplayer gaming challenges to get the highest score. It is panel based, in a similar style to Jeopardy. This desktop app was created with Java and Swing, and is best used in conjunction with a large screen or a projector!

ThrownControllers on GitHub


yelp!help

A friendly alternative to Yelp.com's bookmarking system

Final project for COMS W4170: User Interface Design. Created by myself, Shannon Lee, Siddhi Mittal, and Stephen Somuah, yelp!help is designed to be an easier way to create, search, and filter bookmarks from Yelp. It implements several convenient features that Yelp's bookmarking system lacks, such as parallel filters, additional sort options, and batch select/delete.

Try it out! // View Demo Video


TeapotSun

Fully explorable random solar system generator

Final project for COMS W4160: Computer Graphics. Created in C++ using OpenGL and glut, this simple application generates a random solar system with a sun, 8 planets, and an asteroid belt. Each planet and asteroid is generated at a random distance and has its own size, texture, rotation speed and revolution speed. The system is explorable via first-person shooter style controls, and has keyboard shortcuts for changing speed, toggling orbit paths, and generating a new system.

TeapotSun on GitHub


cats.

A gesture-based image browser

Another project for COMS W4170: User Interface Design. This web app uses a modified version of the $1 unistroke gesture recognizer to browse images of cats. The user simply clicks and drags the mouse over the images and performs any of the available gestures.

Try it out!

Music & YouTube How I spend time on the Internet.

Music is one of my biggest hobbies, both listening and creating. I started playing the piano almost 12 years ago, and have picked up a half-dozen or so instruments along the way. I am a fan of all genres and love to experiment. Nowadays I am spending much of my free time doing music production, both for myself and others. I explain more about what I do in the YouTube section.

All of my music is free to stream and download on my Bandcamp page.

Video is just as big a hobby for me as music, so I wanted to figure out a meaningful way to combine the two. I eventually discovered videosongs: music videos with multiple camera angles and fast-paced edits that show off the entire live recording process. Essentially, a videosong is a is a type of medium with two rules:
- What you see is what you hear (no lip-syncing for instruments or voice)
- If you hear it, at some point you see it (no hidden sounds)

I create videosongs under the moniker goldinsounds, and most of my videos can be found on my YouTube channel of the same name. I produce videos for other channels as well, and can be seen at places like here and here.

My most popular YouTube music endeavor to date is Video Game Cover Week, a two-month project that I completed and released in February 2013. I recorded and produced an EP consisting of 7 songs from popular video games, made a videosong for each, and released one video every day during the week of February 3. Since then, the project has ammassed over 20,000 views on YouTube, over 1600 downloads on Bandcamp, and has been featured on FUSE and the OFFICIAL Tetris Facebook page!
